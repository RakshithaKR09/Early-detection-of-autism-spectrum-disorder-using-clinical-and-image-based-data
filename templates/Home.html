What is Autism?

Autism is a complex neurodevelopmental condition that affects an individual's ability to communicate, interact socially, and process sensory information. It is referred to as a spectrum because the characteristics and severity can vary significantly from one person to another. Some people with autism may struggle with speech, while others may have an extraordinary ability in areas like mathematics, art, or music. The causes of autism are not yet fully understood, but both genetics and environmental factors may play a role in its development.

Individuals with autism often face challenges with social communication and may find it difficult to interpret social cues or engage in typical conversations. However, many people on the autism spectrum can live fulfilling lives with the right support and interventions. Early diagnosis and therapies, such as speech therapy, occupational therapy, and behavioral therapy, can help individuals develop important skills and improve their quality of life.
